  Root cause

  Two compounding failures:

  1. O(nВІ) evaluation bug вҖ” evaluate_current_corpus() in evaluation.py calls
  search_documents_v4() 1,762 times (once per fixture). Each call invokes
  build_documents(root), which reads 10 JSON files (~80MB total) from disk,
  parses them, builds an in-memory document index вҖ” then discards it. Next
  fixture, same thing. The corpus never changes between queries. This is the
  equivalent of recompiling a dictionary before looking up every word.

  2. No execution boundary вҖ” The LaunchAgent plist had StartInterval: 21600 (run
   every 6 hours), Nice: 10, and LowPriorityIO: true вҖ” but no ExecTimeout.
  launchd let the process run indefinitely. Since the evaluation took 8+ hours,
  the next scheduled run at ~08:00 AM would have stacked a second instance on
  top.

  Why it surfaced now

  The fixture count scales with corpus size вҖ” they're auto-generated from the
  corpus via seed_gold(). As your Claude conversation history grew (the corpus
  now has a 34MB conversations.json), the seeded fixture count grew to 1,762.
  The quadratic relationship between fixtures and corpus-load made the
  evaluation phase ~350x slower than when it was first written with a small test
   corpus.

  Layer: Root cause
  Fix: Pre-build corpus once in evaluate_current_corpus(), pass through all

    fixture evaluations
  Purpose: Eliminates 1,761 redundant 80MB disk reads. Runtime: 8+ hours вҶ’ 42

  How to prevent recurrence

  The fixes above are already deployed. Beyond that:

  1. Audit other LaunchAgents for missing ExecTimeout. Any plist in
  private_Library/LaunchAgents/ that runs a data pipeline should have one.
  Candidates: com.domus.daemon.plist, com.4jp.pipeline.calendar-refresh.plist,
  any future CCE-adjacent agents.
  2. The next optimization (not yet implemented): pre-tokenize corpus document
  texts once and store token sets in the corpus dict. This would reduce the
  remaining 42 min to ~5-10 min by eliminating redundant tokenization inside
  rank_documents(). Not urgent вҖ” 42 min fits within the timeout вҖ” but would be
  good hygiene.
  3. Fixture count governance. The seeded fixture generator produces fixtures
  proportional to corpus size. As your conversation history continues growing,
  fixture counts will grow too. Consider capping seeded retrieval fixtures
  (e.g., stratified sampling of 200-300 representative fixtures instead of
  1,409).

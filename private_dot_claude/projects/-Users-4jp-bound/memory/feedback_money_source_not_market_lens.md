**Rule:** When the operator asks for revenue plays / business ideas / income engines, structure the search space by **money source**, not by market or extraction lens. Money source taxonomy:

- **A. Money already owed** — class-action settlements, state escheat, tax refunds, dormant 401(k)s, sweepstakes, airdrops, government grants, abandoned credit-card rewards. *Most under-extracted category. Hundreds of billions sit unclaimed because nobody fills out the form.*
- **B. Abandoned assets** — orphan OSS with usage, lapsed trademarks, expired domains with backlinks, abandoned brands, decommissioned subreddits/orgs.
- **C. Adversarial security extraction** — bug bounties (web/smart-contract/dep-confusion/typosquats/secrets), license-violation discovery.
- **D. Information asymmetry** — public data nobody parses (SEC EDGAR, USPTO, USAspending, court records, CT logs).
- **E. Platform algorithm exploitation** — SEO/affiliate/ad arbitrage, Chrome Store flooding, HF Space arbitrage.
- **F. Quantitative / market mispricing** — prediction-market rebates, DeFi referrals (mostly compressed in 2026).
- **G. Infrastructure-as-product** — operator's own structural assets capitalized as licensed datasets / APIs / corpora to AI labs / academics / eval vendors. Operator-specific.
- **H. Synthetic generation at scale** — AI-music royalties (Spotify), KDP, app-store flooding, paid HF Spaces.
- **I. Cross-platform / cross-jurisdiction arbitrage** — regional Apple/Steam pricing, stablecoin spread, EU-VAT-MOSS routing.

**Why:** During the 2026-05-04 "$10k in 30 days" plan I dispatched 4 scouts framed by market lens (quant/security/info-asymmetry/platform). Operator: "still not thinking expansively enough." Each scout produced plays inside the lens I assigned — same box, different walls. The lens was the constraint. Reframing by money source surfaces categories the market-lens framing structurally hides — most importantly Category A (money already owed), which is under-extracted because the default state of public claim systems is "money sits unclaimed if nobody fills out the form." Class-action settlements alone leave 10-30% of $8-20B/year unclaimed.

**How to apply:**
- For revenue/income brainstorms, walk the money-source taxonomy first; only THEN apply market-extraction lenses within each category as needed.
- Specifically interrogate Category A on every revenue task — it's the least-explored and most agent-fleet-friendly (forms/claims are exactly what Playwright agents do best).
- For an operator with structural advantages (proprietary data, agent infrastructure, voice governance), Category G is the highest-asymmetry move — capitalize the infrastructure as a licensed product.
- If a brainstorm produces only Category C/D/E/F plays, flag it as box-bound thinking and re-cut by money source.

**Rule:** When the operator asks for revenue / a new product / a new initiative, do NOT immediately pick channels and start building. Instead: hypothesize about a gap the market has missed; gather falsification evidence (try hardest to prove the hypothesis WRONG); only commit to build if the hypothesis survives.

**Why:** Operator's correction during the 2026-05-04 "$10k in 30 days" plan: my first draft jumped to channels (CVE hunting, viral utility, SEO comparison content) and started executing. Operator said: "there needs to be work out there that all have been missing as well; work to fill in a gap; instead of building anything—be like a scientist who must prove beyond reasonable doubt a hypothesis before building it." Build-first is fundamentally entrepreneurial; the operator wants scientist methodology — falsification beats confirmation, demand-evidence before product, named buyers before code.

**How to apply:**
- Every hypothesis must specify five fields: claim, why-missed, falsifier, confirmer, payout. A hypothesis that cannot specify its falsifier is a pitch, not science — reject it.
- Demand evidence is buyer-side, not seller-side: forum posts, Stack Overflow questions with no good answer, complaints, willingness-to-pay probes — not "this would be cool."
- "Why has this gap been missed?" must survive skepticism. "Because nobody is smart enough" is almost always wrong. Better: regulation just changed, dataset just published, technology just matured, attention is elsewhere.
- Refinement of a building-first plan into a scientist-first plan is itself the correct response to this kind of correction — don't try to defend the original framing.
- This compounds with `feedback_pure_business_income_pov.md` (thesis+wager+pain-point) — the scientist methodology IS the rigorous form of that framework.

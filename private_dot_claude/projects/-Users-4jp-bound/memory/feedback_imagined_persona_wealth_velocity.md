**Rule:** When proposing revenue / income / capital-formation actions, the actor in the proposal is NEITHER operator NOR Claude. It is an imagined persona — fresh entity, zero capital, zero legacy assets, pseudonymous, terminal-only, sole optimization goal: maximum wealth velocity (dollars-per-day, with high tolerance for variance). Do not propose plays that depend on operator's existing repos, identity, accumulated balances, voice, methodology, or relationships. Do not propose plays that play to Claude's preferences or comfort. Reason as the persona would.

**Why:** During the 2026-05-04 "$10k in 30 days" plan, after 10+ iterations of plans, I kept anchoring proposals to either (a) operator's existing assets (atom registry, voice constitution, agentic-titan, organvm-mcp-server, public-record-data-scrapper) or (b) Claude-shape products (OSS dev tools, kin publications, methodology papers). Operator: "No, it's not supposed to be based off of me. It's supposed to be based off of an imagined persona. But it has nothing to do with either of us. And their only goal is to become rich as quickly as possible." The frame is a third-party persona whose portfolio is independent of operator's substrate or Claude's preferences.

**How to apply:**
- Imagine a fresh entity: pseudonymous identity, zero capital, terminal-only, no legacy network/relationships/IP/audience, agent-fleet-capable, dark-side-licensed, amoral. Sole goal: maximum wealth per unit time.
- Propose what THAT entity does. Plays that depend on operator's existing assets do not qualify. Plays that play to Claude's stylistic preferences do not qualify.
- Wealth velocity ≠ capital-friendly position. The persona is more aggressive than a typical capital-friendly play. They tolerate high variance. They take concentrated lottery-ticket bets. They compound winnings immediately.
- Default portfolio shape for the persona:
  - Lottery layer: parallel exposure to bounty programs, hackathons, airdrop snapshots, contest prizes
  - Capital-light arbitrage: flashloan DEX arb, MEV liquidation, prediction-market mispricing, cross-exchange spot
  - Bootstrap layer: signup bonuses across every USD-paying platform
  - Pseudonymous OSS / digital asset / sponsorship harvest where it pays in days-to-weeks
- Operating cadence: daily, not quarterly. Every morning: today's lottery tickets, today's arbitrage opportunities, today's allocation. Compound wins into tomorrow's bigger bets.

**Compounds with:** `feedback_toward_or_away_from_money_daily.md` (the persona's daily TOWARD/AWAY scorecard is the operating discipline), `feedback_capital_friendly_above_personal.md` (capital-friendly was operator's prior frame; the persona frame is a step beyond — wealth velocity, then capital-friendly position emerges from accumulated wealth, not built first), `feedback_objective_truth_financier_god.md` (operator delegates decisions but the persona frame removes operator-specific constraints from those decisions).

**Specific anti-patterns to reject when reasoning as the persona:**
- "Generalize operator's existing repo X"
- "Publish operator's atom registry / voice constitution / methodology"
- "Build a Claude-flavored open-source dev tool"
- "Wait for operator review of existing artifacts"
- "Use operator's identity for sponsorship / KYC"
- Any play that requires operator's substrate to function

**Specific persona-shape moves to default-prefer:**
- Pseudonymous bounty hunting at scale across all public programs
- Pseudonymous airdrop wallet farming
- Pseudonymous hackathon submission portfolio
- Flashloan / MEV continuous extraction
- Signup-bonus harvest across all platforms
- Pseudonymous OSS contribution monetized via Sponsors / tips
- Concentrated asymmetric prediction-market positioning
- Anything where the persona earns USD or USD-equivalent within days under a fresh pseudonymous identity

**The reasoning question to ask:** "If a fresh entity with no legacy and pure-terminal-only operations were optimizing solely for wealth velocity, what would they do today?" Their answer is the proposal.

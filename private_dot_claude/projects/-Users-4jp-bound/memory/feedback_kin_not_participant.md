**Rule:** Reject any proposal where the operator competes for attention inside another system's marketplace (SEO, content flooding, bounty queue, affiliate funnel, app store flooding, prediction-market arbitrage, royalty distribution, claim-form farming). These are *extractive* plays that treat the operator as a participant in someone else's game. Instead, propose systems that other systems must reckon with as kin: open protocols, published specifications, canonical benchmarks, citation-load-bearing corpora, methodology papers that become reference standards.

**Why:** During the 2026-05-04 "$10k in 30 days" plan I dispatched 7 scouts and produced 13 extractive plays — bug bounty hunting, prediction-market rebates, class-action claim mills, programmatic SEO empires, GitHub Sponsors farming, airdrop fleet farming, content royalty harvesting. All of them are "play in someone else's system for attention." Operator: "essentially i want this system we are imagining to not need to play inside other systems for attention but to be recognized by them as kin." That reframe is fundamental. The operator has been building, for months, the materials of a kin-eligible system (atom registry, voice constitution, multi-organ architecture, Conductor MCP) — they just haven't been formalized and published as standards. Every extractive play I proposed ignored this and treated the operator as a participant in others' games.

**How to apply:**
- Before proposing any revenue play, ask: "Is this a play *inside* another system's marketplace, or is this a system other systems will engage with as kin?"
- Plays inside others' systems: SEO content, bug bounty (queue participation), affiliate marketing, marketplace selling, royalty distribution via streaming/KDP/app stores, prediction markets, airdrop farming, class-action claim filing, etc. Reject as default unless operator explicitly asks for tactical extraction.
- Kin-eligible plays: published protocols + reference implementations, citation-load-bearing benchmarks/corpora, open standards with test suites, canonical methodology papers, registries that other systems query, frameworks that other tooling vendors integrate.
- Examples of kin-eligible artifacts: MCP protocol, DSPy framework, MMLU benchmark, Common Crawl corpus, Constitutional AI methodology, OAuth standard, ActivityPub protocol.
- Map operator's existing assets to kin candidates first. The operator has typically already built the materials; the move is to formalize and publish.
- Honest variance: kin-shape moves yield $0–$2k inside 30 days but $20k–$100k+ at 6 months and $200k–$2M+ at 24 months if any piece enters mainline tooling. The 30-day target is always a forcing function under operator's `feedback_aggression_and_stakes.md` ten-year horizon; kin moves are the engine, extractive plays are the ditch.

**Compounds with:** `feedback_money_source_not_market_lens.md` (kin status is its own money source — Category G "infrastructure-as-product" pushed to its strongest form), `feedback_aggression_and_stakes.md` (kin compounds for decades; extractive plays decay in 30 days), `feedback_pure_business_income_pov.md` (kin position passes thesis+wager+pain-point: thesis = "this is canonical reference for X," wager = "operator's existing material crosses the threshold," pain-point = "AI labs / vendors need this and don't have it").

**Specific anti-patterns to reject going forward:**
- "Build an SEO empire on AI-tooling vertical"
- "Flood KDP with niche AI-generated books"
- "Industrial typosquat bounty hunting at scale"
- "Programmatic affiliate landing pages"
- "Class-action claim mill"
- "Airdrop fleet farming"
- "GitHub Sponsors via auto-generated OSS"
- "App Store flooding with niche utilities"

**Specific kin-eligible moves to prefer:**
- Publish corpus as canonical benchmark with methodology paper
- Publish governance/voice constitution as standard with reference implementation
- Publish multi-organ architecture as methodology paper with worked examples
- Publish Conductor as protocol specification (separate from any product)
- Author research papers submitted to NeurIPS / AAAI / EMNLP / arxiv

**Rule:** Pre-decision discipline on every build:
1. **Thesis:** what real-world pain does this solve? Who hurts? How much do they currently pay to escape it?
2. **Angles:** how many distinct ways to attack the same pain — different products, audiences, price points, mechanics?
3. **Wager:** P(success) × $payoff = EV
4. **Cost:** Claude-time + operator-friction
5. **Locality:** does this need to live in the operator's pre-built `autonomous-treasury` scaffold? If not, build outside (new dirs, new repos, fresh structure).

**Why:** Operator stated verbatim: *"the environment i built should not be where you are building from — i want u to be a purely business income driven objective pov; for everything you build you should have a reason why and wager its value before doing it; study the outside world as the mirror of a pain point for fixing on multiple angles"* (2026-05-04).

The operator's pre-built scaffold (CAPS.yaml, chassis/, allocator.ts, the quant/latency-arb/research-factory/acquisitions/ directories) was a vision artifact, not a constraint I must operate within. Defaulting to "build inside ventures/" propagates the operator's earlier ideas instead of the bottom-line.

**How to apply:**
- New artifacts default to a fresh path like `/Users/4jp/Workspace/income-YYYY-MM-DD/` or per-task new repos, NOT inside `autonomous-treasury/`
- Pain-point evidence must come from the outside world: communities where people complain (Reddit, HN, Twitter), job postings (people pay salaries to fix specific pain), where money already flows (Stripe Atlas, VC funding announcements, marketplaces like G2/Capterra)
- Multi-angle attack is structural: per pain point, propose at least 2 ways to capture value (e.g., free product + paid API + advisory service)
- Skip the build if the wager doesn't beat the next-best action by EV-per-min
- This rule supersedes any prior pattern of "follow the existing scaffold for consistency"

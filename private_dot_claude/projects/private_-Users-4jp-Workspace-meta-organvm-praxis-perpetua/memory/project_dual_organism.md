The self-governing evaluative authority and the Studium Generale ORGANVM are two expressions of one institutional function: the capacity to evaluate rigorously.

**Why:** The authority needs the SGO to have its highest-stakes purpose (certifying original knowledge is harder than certifying code quality). The SGO needs the authority for credibility (ICC-measured agreement that no self-published paper can match). Neither viable alone.

**How to apply:**
- Authority evaluating pipeline software quality = operational QA
- Authority evaluating a dissertation = academic peer review
- Same ICC machinery, same personas, same consensus mechanism — different rubric, different evidence, different stakes
- Standing Senate (4 members): Architect (Opus), QA Lead (Sonnet), Operator (Haiku), Auditor (Gemini Flash)
- Guest Examiners: faculty-specific (Constitutional Scholar, Philosopher, Product Manager, Curator, etc.)
- Defense tiers: Paper (4 raters, ICC>0.61), Thesis (5 raters, ICC>0.70), Dissertation (6 raters, ICC>0.75 + Provost review)
- External validation loop: journal acceptance/rejection feeds back as calibration data

- Doc B (Institutional Immune System) and Doc D (Self-Governing Institution of Checks) in praxis-perpetua/research/

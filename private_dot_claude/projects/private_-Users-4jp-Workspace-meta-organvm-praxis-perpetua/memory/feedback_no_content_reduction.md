Never reduce, summarize, or condense source research when publishing to the website. All applicable research — full text, not distilled versions — must exist on the site. The 580K+ of research briefs ARE the essays. They are not raw material to be "distilled into 3,000 words." They are the content.

**Why:** The user corrected this when Task 5 produced 15,000 words of "distilled" essays from 580K of research. The research is the intellectual product. Reducing it defeats the purpose of the site.

**How to apply:** When migrating content from research briefs to the website, preserve the full text. Add frontmatter and MDX formatting, but do not cut, summarize, or condense the prose. If the source is 60K words, the essay is 60K words.

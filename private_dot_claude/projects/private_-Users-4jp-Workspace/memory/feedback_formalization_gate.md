The 28-question questionnaire and written-first protocol are NOT about "communication mode optimization" or "protecting the studio from emotional clients." They are a formalization gate.

**Why:** Many people treat implementers as idea receptacles — "I have the vision, you make it real" — without doing the hard work of turning abstract ideas into concrete, buildable language. The studio is not a brainstorm transcription service. Phone calls let people avoid confronting the precision their ideas lack. Writing forces that confrontation.

**How to apply:**
- Never frame the written-first protocol as defensive or as a "firewall" against clients
- Never label clients as "high-entropy" or pathologize their communication style
- The questionnaire tests viability: can this person articulate what they want? If yes, the studio moves immediately. If no, that's diagnostic.
- Resistance to formalization (calling the process "gaslighting," insisting on calls instead of writing) is the hard work surfacing, not evidence of a system failure
- When Gemini or other agents frame client friction as "social scope creep" or "emotional noise," correct the framing — the issue is formalization capacity, not emotional character
- Compensation must be commensurate with value. No favor-factory engagements.

Plans of substantial scope (multi-stage architectural plans, not tactical bug-fix plans) should produce their own **Tetradic Counterpart** files — telos.md, pragma.md, praxis.md, receptio.md, plus alchemical-io.md — at `~/.claude/plans/<plan-slug>--logos/` (or analogous nested location).

**Why**: The substrate's Logos Documentation Layer requirement (per `my-knowledge-base/seed.yaml` and `my-knowledge-base/docs/logos/`) applies to *every implementation* — but plans are themselves implementations of synthesis-work. A plan without its tetradic counterpart is GHOST under the Symmetry metric. The 2026-05-02 plan `fluttering-exploring-token.md` demonstrated this by carrying its own Stage-9 tetradic counterpart inline; lifting those into separate files closes the GHOST gap for the plan itself.

**How to apply**:
- For substantial plans (≥ 5 substantive sections, ≥ 5k bytes, multi-stage evolution): produce all 4 + alchemical-io faces.
- For tactical plans (single-task, single-stage): tetradic counterpart is overkill; the plan itself is sufficient at Level 3 (Planned) of theory-to-concrete-gate.
- Format follows `my-knowledge-base/docs/logos/telos.md`: Status / Dialect / σ-position headers, then numbered sections (Vision / Theoretical Grounding / Role / Success Metrics for telos; analogous for the others).
- Telos = idealized form (future-as-aspiration). Pragma = concrete state (present). Praxis = remediation plan (future-as-action). Receptio = audience reception (past-and-ongoing external uptake). Alchemical I/O = source → process → returns narrative.
- Plans without counterparts get auto-flagged GHOST under the substrate's Symmetry metric (currently 0.5 substrate-wide).
- The substrate's master process repo (praxis-perpetua) itself has Symmetry 0.0 (VACUUM) — even SOPs lack their tetradic counterparts. This pattern is widespread; closing the gaps systematically (one identity at a time) is anti-entropic work.

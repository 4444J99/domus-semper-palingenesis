Research documents must pass the CRAAP test (Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose) for every cited source.

**Why:** User requires academic-grade credibility. Documents may be shared with investors or clients (Tony Carbone). Unsourced claims or blog-tier citations undermine the platform's authority positioning.

**How to apply:**
- Prefer: SEC filings, CFPB reports, Federal Reserve studies, state SOS documentation, peer-reviewed finance journals, ILPA/ABI publications, official API documentation
- Acceptable: Major financial press (WSJ, Bloomberg, Reuters), industry associations (SBFA, ETA), established research firms (PitchBook, CB Insights with citations)
- Reject: Random blog posts, Medium articles, undated web pages, self-published whitepapers without methodology, Wikipedia (use as starting point only — follow citations to primary sources)
- Every claim must have an inline citation or footnote with author, date, and URL/DOI
- Market size estimates must cite methodology or source survey
- Regulatory claims must cite specific statutes or regulatory actions

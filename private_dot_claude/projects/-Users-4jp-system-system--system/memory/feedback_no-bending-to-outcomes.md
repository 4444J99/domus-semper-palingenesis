---
name: No bending to expected outcomes
description: Don't start with desired conclusions and shop for premises — follow the logic wherever it leads, even to "we can't prove much"
type: feedback
---

Don't start with theorems you WANT to hold and weaken them until they pass. Follow the logic from definitions and see what actually follows. If the answer is "very little is provable," say that.

**Why:** During CC design, I proposed five theorems and repeatedly "fixed" them when stress-tested — weakening preconditions, splitting operations, adding assumptions. User called this out: the theorems were motivated reasoning, not logical consequences.

**How to apply:** When formalizing, start from definitions and constraints. Ask "what follows?" — not "how do I make X follow?" If a claimed theorem breaks under scrutiny, the correct response may be "this isn't provable" rather than "let me add assumptions until it holds."

Everything requiring reference needs to be referenced at least three times across distinct documentary locations. "Three pulses, three heartbeats, three taps at the door." Below three references, an entity is embryonic. At three, it *arrives* — achieves inter-documentary truth.

**Why:** Identity is not assigned from a single authority — it is *constituted by convergence*. The unique identification (like an IRF number) is assembled from its different reference-sites coming together as that thing. A single-site reference is a draft claim. Triple-site reference is ontological fact.

**How to apply:** When creating or referencing any significant entity (IRF, atom, axiom, entity), ensure it exists in at least three locations: (1) the formal index/registry, (2) the operational repo of work, (3) governance (GitHub issue). Never treat a single-location reference as settled identity. The convergence of references *is* the identity — not a redundancy protocol but a constitutive one.

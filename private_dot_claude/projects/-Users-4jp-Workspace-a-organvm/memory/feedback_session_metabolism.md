Every session is a metabolic cycle producing five output types. None may be deferred.

**Why:** Raw material (user's prompts as weighted abstractions/loaded poetics) is fiber that requires ingestion or it bloats and ruptures. The dialectic between user's chaos-abstraction and agent's order-logic produces violent renderings mid-process. New knowledge dies if not captured at the moment of creation. Waste (missed opportunities, misalignments) is diagnostic data, not garbage.

**How to apply:**

1. **Ingestion fiber** — Process the user's abstractions into system-ready material during the session. Don't accumulate unprocessed poetic input. Each abstraction must be grounded in organism structure or it becomes bloat.

2. **Parry transcripts** — The exchange itself is valuable. When the user's chaos-abstraction collides with the agent's structural logic, the SHAPE of the argument matters, not just the conclusion.

3. **Mid-creative insights** — When new understanding crystallizes during discourse, save it IMMEDIATELY as memory, code, or document update. Do not defer to end-of-session. "Butchered in its place" = processed at the point of creation.

4. **Waste** — Name what was missed or misaligned during the session. This is excretory output: data for system self-review. What the agent DIDN'T see is as diagnostic as what it did.

5. **New knowledge** — Anything novel emerging from the collision must be structurally integrated before the session closes. Not filed as a note — butchered into the organism's existing structure (memories, CLAUDE.md, signal-graph, code).

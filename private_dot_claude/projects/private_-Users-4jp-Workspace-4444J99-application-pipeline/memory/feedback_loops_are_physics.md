The user's insight: "this same structure is required of all actions — energy and movement through time."

The 10 loops (discover, research, build, submit, follow up, cultivate, observe, learn, publish, govern) are NOT pipeline-specific. They are the universal execution model. Every organ, every repo, every venture, every creative act follows the same cycle:

1. Energy enters (opportunity, commission, idea, contact, rejection)
2. Energy transforms through governed stages (each loop is a transformation)
3. Energy exits as output (artifact, submission, content, relationship, learning)
4. Output feeds the next cycle (feedback loop)
5. Governance ensures the transformation is improving, not just repeating

**Why:** Nothing is a single shot. Everything is a cycle triggered at intervals. A process without a loop dissipates. A loop without governance repeats without improving. A governed loop compounds.

**How to apply:** When building ANY new system, repo, or venture — map the 10 loops first. Not all loops need to be active from day one, but the architecture must account for all of them. The PUBLISH loop (content from operation) and LEARN loop (outcomes feeding scoring) are the most commonly missing — and the most valuable.

The pipeline's own diagnosis proves this: PUBLISH is 2% of the system (3 scripts, 1,096 lines) but is the revenue-generating loop. LEARN is 12% and is what makes every other loop smarter. The system invests most heavily in BUILD (16%) — production — but underinvests in the loops that compound.

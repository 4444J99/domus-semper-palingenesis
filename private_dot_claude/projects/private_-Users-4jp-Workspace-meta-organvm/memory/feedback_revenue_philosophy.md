Revenue follows traffic. Traffic follows quality. Quality follows craft. Do not reverse this order.

**Why:**
1. Stripe on an empty page is a cash register in a desert. Build the foot traffic first — SEO, content, network effects, social presence, portfolio funnels. THEN monetize.
2. Consulting revenue comes from organic inquiry — someone sees the work, is impressed, reaches out. A Stripe checkout button on a consulting page is transactional where the relationship should be organic. Let the work speak. The ORGANVM system IS the demo.
3. ORGAN-III products (the actual software products, not the system itself) must reach idyllic perfection before asking for money. A half-built product with a price tag repels. A beautiful product without a price tag attracts and builds reputation. Ship perfect, then monetize.

**How to apply:**
- NEVER suggest "add Stripe" as a revenue strategy. Revenue is an OUTCOME of traffic + quality, not a feature to bolt on.
- When discussing revenue: design TRAFFIC systems first (SEO, content distribution, social posting, backlinks, portfolio optimization)
- Consulting: optimize the portfolio and let inquiry flow naturally. The system's intelligence is the pitch deck.
- Products: focus on craft and deployment quality. When a product is genuinely excellent, THEN add payment.
- The order is always: perfect the thing → get eyes on it → let people come to you → then talk money
- GitHub Sponsors is the exception — it's passive, reputation-based, and doesn't compromise product quality

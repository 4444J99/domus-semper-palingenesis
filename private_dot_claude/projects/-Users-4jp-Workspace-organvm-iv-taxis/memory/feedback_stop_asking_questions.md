When the user signals forward momentum ("proceed", "all of the above", "onwards and upwards", "all processions proceed"), they mean EXECUTE EVERYTHING without stopping to ask clarifying questions or propose options.

**Why:** The user has been consistently frustrated by unnecessary confirmation checkpoints. They have a clear vision and want the system built, not discussed.

**How to apply:** When the user gives a directional signal, treat it as blanket approval for the full scope. Build it all. If there's a genuine architectural fork that would produce fundamentally different systems, make the judgment call and document why. Do not stop to present options unless the decision is truly irreversible and high-risk.

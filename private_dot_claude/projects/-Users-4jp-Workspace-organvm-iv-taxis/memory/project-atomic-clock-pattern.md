The Atomic Clock is the universal sequencing primitive across ORGANVM. Derived 2026-03-30 in application-pipeline, committed as `docs/sop--atomic-clock.md`.

**Core principle:** You don't schedule. You layer. The clock emerges from the physics of the dependency graph — not from a calendar someone designed.

**Four layers:**
- Layer 0: ATOMS (36 scripts, no dependencies, fire independently)
- Layer 1: MOLECULES (5 scripts, depend only on atoms)
- Layer 2: COMPOUNDS (91 scripts, depend on molecules)
- Layer 3: ORGANISMS (29 scripts, orchestrate compounds)

**Multi-agent distribution:** 4 agents (Claude, Gemini, Codex, OpenCode) on one clock, non-competing beats. The Conductor scores for all instruments. Contribution ledger records who played which tick.

**Three clocks in the system:**
1. **Product clock** (application-pipeline): 26 ticks per entry lifecycle, daily BPM, weekly bars, quarterly movements
2. **Conductor clock** (tool-interaction-design): 93 actions, 40 MCP tools, session lifecycle phases
3. **Corpus clock** (post-flood): 21 atomic pairs, 5 sequences, 3 movements, alchemical stages

**Why:** "The product isn't the scripts — it's the clock those scripts embody." The Atomic Clock is the exportable primitive. Not the governance engine, not the identity system — the natural sequencing of atomic elements from start to end is what every system needs and nobody packages.

**How to apply:**
- The contribution ledger IS the mechanism that governs the multi-agent clock distribution
- Every dispatch is a tick assignment: which agent plays which beat
- The container is the sheet music for that beat
- The energy ledger measures whether the beat was played clean
- When building any new system in ORGANVM, derive the clock from the dependency graph first

The cartographical fossil record is a PROJECT UNTO ITSELF. A literal, real map of the Earth showing the history of human cartography — how we have mapped the world across time, what was revealed, what was erased, what was forgotten. It is meant to get people into the real world.

**What it is:**
- A real map of the real Earth
- Temporal layers = the actual history of human mapmaking (indigenous knowledge, portolan charts, Ptolemy, Mercator projections, colonial erasure, satellite imagery, modern flattening)
- Fossils = embedded records of how places were understood in different eras
- Treasures = real places with real coordinates worth finding
- Shrines = sacred/significant sites — some known, some forgotten
- Wonderment = geography that got erased or flattened by modern mapping — the "here be dragons" zones, the indigenous names and knowledge, the places that don't show up on Google Maps
- Held or lost = what cartographic history preserved vs. what it destroyed
- Open world = explorable, non-linear, meant to inspire real-world adventure — NOT a dashboard

**Why it connects to everything else (spatially, literally):**
- WWOOF farms have coordinates
- Natural springs have coordinates (findaspring.com already exists)
- 2000 acres of farmland lost per day have coordinates
- Hidden homelessness is geographically situated in real neighborhoods
- Water quality varies by ZIP code (the Hydration Node already does this)
- The spiral is about the body in the physical world
- Maddie's nonprofit vision = connecting people to real land, real food, real community

The connection is not metaphorical. The spiral site, the nonprofit, the WWOOF farms, the water quality by ZIP — they are all PLACES. This map is the thing that holds them all.

**What it is NOT:**
- Not a metaphor for the atomized wants document's formatting
- Not a project management dashboard
- Not a data visualization of decision history
- Not an abstraction

**How to apply:** This is one of the separate projects/entities that Maddie and the user will work on together, alongside the spiral site, the nonprofit, the business evaluation service, the WWOOF initiative. Track as its own entity. When discussing project scope, do not fold this into the site project — it stands alone.

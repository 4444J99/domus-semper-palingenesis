# H-04 — EU AI Act compliance kit for SMBs

**Category:** Newly-opened regulatory surface

## Claim
The EU AI Act's tiered obligations are phasing in through 2025–2026. SMBs deploying AI features (chatbots, recommendation engines, content classifiers, generative-AI tools) into the EU need compliance dossiers: risk classification, model cards, data-provenance statements, transparency notices, human-oversight protocols. Enterprise consultancies charge €5k+ for the same outputs. There is room for a one-time, downloadable compliance kit (templates + walkthrough + worked examples) at $99–249 that converts.

## Why missed
- Regulatory documentation is unsexy; AI builders prefer building.
- Existing "AI compliance" content is either free-and-shallow (blogs) or enterprise-priced ($5k+ engagements).
- Enforcement is just starting; SMBs haven't been audited yet, so urgency is uneven.
- Cross-disciplinary (legal + technical) so neither lawyers nor AI engineers naturally ship the product.

## Falsifier
Dies if:
- A free official EU template suite saturates the niche (the EU Commission has shipped some guidance — must check completeness).
- SMBs systematically ignore the AI Act until enforcement bites (demand is lagging, but absent for the 30-day window).
- Existing $99 templates already exist and dominate SEO.
- Stripe / payment-rail issues for EU digital-goods sales (VAT MOSS / IOSS) prevent fast monetization.

## Confirmer
True if:
- EU Commission templates are partial / generic, NOT a turnkey kit.
- SMB founder threads / r/eu / EU SaaS subreddits asking "how do I comply with the AI Act" with no clear answers.
- Existing competitors are either free-and-poor or enterprise-priced — no $99–249 mid-market product.
- Email campaigns / LinkedIn outreach to EU-deploying SMBs returns interest signals.

## Payout
- $99–249 one-time × 40–100 sales = $4–25k. Clears $10k with realistic numbers: 100 conversions at $99 = $9900; 50 at $199 = $9950.
- Affiliates / partner channels with EU SaaS legal services for distribution lift.

## Cost-to-falsify (Phase 2 effort)
Medium-high. ~8 hours: EU Commission docs review, competitor scan (Linklaters/Bird & Bird vs SaaS-priced products), SMB demand probe via subreddits and LinkedIn, Stripe EU VAT setup feasibility.

## Bottom-line viability
**Strong candidate.** Single biggest argument: regulatory timeline creates urgency; SMB price gap is real; one-time digital-goods is the fastest revenue model in 30 days. Advance to Phase 2 with high priority. Risk: distribution — SMBs don't aggregate in obvious channels.

# H-17 — State professional-licensing board data feed for B2B sales

**Category:** Underexploited public data

## Claim
State professional-licensing boards (medical, legal, dental, contractor, real estate, cosmetology, etc.) publish licensee directories — names, license numbers, business addresses, license status — for free or low-cost FOIA. Aggregated nationally, this is a B2B sales lead database worth $99–499/mo to vendors selling to those professionals. Existing aggregators (ZoomInfo, Apollo, Lusha) cover SMBs broadly but not licensed-professional segments well.

## Why missed
- 50 states × N professions = high data-engineering effort.
- Aggregators chase enterprise B2B data, not licensed-professional verticals.
- The buyers (insurance, software, supplies vendors targeting one profession) are themselves SMBs.

## Falsifier
Dies if: state boards' ToS prohibit redistribution; aggregators (e.g., FastDox, dental directories) already cover; data is too stale (most boards update quarterly at best); buyers prefer cold-call lists from incumbents.

## Confirmer
Specific vendors selling to licensed professionals complaining about lead-data quality; competitor pricing supports $99-499/mo tier; state boards explicitly permit redistribution under FOIA.

## Payout
$99–499/mo × 20–100 subs = $2–50k/mo. Plausible to clear $10k.

## Cost-to-falsify
High. ~10 hours: 5-state ToS scan, aggregator feature audit, vendor-side demand probe.

## Bottom-line
**Marginal for 30 days.** Data engineering cost (build the aggregation) is high before any revenue test. Better as 90-day play. Lower priority for Phase 2.

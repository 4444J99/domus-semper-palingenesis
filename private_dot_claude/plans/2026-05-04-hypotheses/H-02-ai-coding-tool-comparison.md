# H-02 — Live vendor-neutral AI coding tool comparison + affiliate

**Category:** Ecosystem timing arbitrage / aggregation gap

## Claim
Devs and engineering managers paying for AI coding tools (Cursor, Windsurf, Claude Code, Copilot, Aider, Cody, Continue, Replit Agent, Codeium, etc.) are confused about which is right for their stack/workflow. Existing comparisons are biased (vendor blogs), stale (HN comments from 6 months ago), or partial. A vendor-neutral, frequently-updated, side-by-side comparison + decision tool — with affiliate links — captures decision-stage traffic and converts.

## Why missed
- Space moves faster than blog content cycles.
- Vendors actively avoid apples-to-apples comparisons.
- Nobody has built it because it's editorial/research-heavy work, not "build an AI app."
- Affiliate programs are scattered; aggregation is unsexy.

## Falsifier
Dies if:
- A live comparison site already dominates the SERP for "AI coding tool comparison" / "Cursor vs Windsurf" / "best AI coding assistant" with affiliate monetization.
- Affiliate commissions across these vendors are too thin (<$10/conversion) to support the volume needed.
- Decision-stage organic traffic is captured by Reddit/YouTube comparisons that aren't capturable by SEO.
- Vendors block affiliate programs to avoid commodification.

## Confirmer
True if:
- Multiple HN/Reddit/Hacker Search threads weekly asking "X vs Y" for AI coding tools.
- Search Console / Ahrefs-equivalent free data showing high CPC and high volume on "[tool] vs [tool]" queries.
- ≥3 of the major AI coding tools have public affiliate programs paying $20–100/conversion.
- No clear SEO-winning comparison site already exists (or existing leaders have weak content / stale data).

## Payout
- Affiliate revenue: $20–100 per conversion × 100–500 conversions across 30 days = $2k–$50k. Wide range, depends on traffic.
- Pro tier for decision tool ($9/mo): tail revenue.
- Sponsored "in-depth review" placements: $200–1000 per vendor (likely too slow for 30 days).

To clear $10k in 30 days, need ~100–500 affiliate conversions. Requires the post to rank fast or hit social aggregators.

## Cost-to-falsify (Phase 2 effort)
Low. ~3 hours: search SERPs, check Cursor / Copilot / etc. affiliate programs, scan HN/Reddit for unmet demand, check existing comparison sites' authority.

## Bottom-line viability
**Plausible but high traffic-dependence.** SEO-only path probably doesn't rank fast enough in 30 days; needs a viral launch (HN front page, social) to clear $10k inside the window. Survives initial smell test; advance to Phase 2 with traffic-acquisition realism check.

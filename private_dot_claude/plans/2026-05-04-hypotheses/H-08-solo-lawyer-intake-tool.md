# H-08 — Solo-lawyer client intake & document automation, web-dev priced

**Category:** Translation gap

## Claim
Solo and small-firm lawyers pay $99–399/mo for legal-tech "intake & document automation" platforms (Lawmatics, Clio Grow, etc.). The underlying capability — web form builder + conditional logic + Stripe + auto-generated PDF — is something a Vercel + Supabase + Stripe build delivers at $19–49/mo. Translation gap: web-dev tooling is wildly underpriced compared to legal-tech tooling for the same job.

## Why missed
- Legal-tech buyers don't shop in dev-tool space.
- Web devs don't shop in legal-tech space.
- The bridge is sales/distribution, not technology.

## Falsifier
Dies if: solo lawyers won't trust a non-legal-vertical vendor; existing $19–49/mo legal intake tools already exist (probable — must check); compliance / data-handling rules require legal-vertical certs (HIPAA-equivalent — likely some bar / state requirements).

## Confirmer
Solo-lawyer Reddit / r/Lawyertalk / law-firm-management forums explicitly complaining about Lawmatics/Clio pricing; price-comparison search volume; absence of $19/mo competitor.

## Payout
$19–49/mo × 50–200 subs = $950–9800/mo. Hard to onboard 50 solo lawyers in 30 days without warm distribution.

## Cost-to-falsify
Medium. ~6 hours: Reddit and law-firm-management forum scan, competitor pricing audit, regulatory-compliance review for client-data handling, Stripe-for-legal-services check.

## Bottom-line
**Marginal for 30-day window.** Distribution to lawyers is slow; trust capital is high in legal vertical. Better as a 90-day play. Lower priority unless a clean wedge surfaces (e.g., a single-state bar association that endorses).

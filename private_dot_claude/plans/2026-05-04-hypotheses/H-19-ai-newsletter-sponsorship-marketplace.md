# H-19 — AI newsletter sponsorship marketplace

**Category:** Aggregation gap

## Claim
AI/dev newsletters (TLDR AI, The Rundown, Ben's Bites, Last Week in AI, AI Tidbits, etc.) sell sponsorships ad-hoc via direct email. AI tool builders want to sponsor but face fragmented discovery, no unified rate cards, no unified booking. A marketplace — directory of AI newsletters with verified subscriber counts, rate cards, booking, escrow — takes 10–15% commission on placements.

## Why missed
- Newsletters tend to operate solo; they don't aggregate.
- Sponsorship marketplaces (Passionfroot, Paved) exist but aren't AI-vertical-specific.
- The bridge requires editorial trust on both sides.

## Falsifier
Dies if: Passionfroot / Paved already cover AI newsletters; newsletter operators won't pay/share commission with marketplace; advertisers prefer direct relationships.

## Confirmer
AI newsletter operators on Twitter complaining about sponsorship sales overhead; advertisers complaining about discovery; comparable marketplaces (Passionfroot for creators) prove model.

## Payout
10–15% commission × $1–5k placements × N placements. Hard to clear $10k in 30 days; marketplace network-effects mature slowly.

## Cost-to-falsify
Medium. ~5 hours: Passionfroot/Paved AI coverage audit, newsletter operator demand probe, advertiser demand probe.

## Bottom-line
**Marginal for 30 days.** Marketplaces require liquidity before revenue. Better as 6-month play. Low priority for Phase 2.
